APPENDIX A. VARIABLE DEFINITIONS

Variables Definition

ADEPft Sales dependence minus cost dependence, where sales dependence is calculated as sales to the buyer divided by
total sales of the supplier, and cost dependence is calculated as purchases from the supplier divided by the
total cost of goods sold of the buyer. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be
converted into the firm level.

AdjARSft + 1 Trade receivables over net sales minus the industry median of trade receivables over net sales at t + 1.

Ageft Firm age.

Age f The between-firm component of Ageft, which is the average Ageft across all fiscal years for each firm.

Cf,t-1 Cash plus marketable securities in year t − 1, adjusted for inflation to 2011.

CANft 1 if the relationship has a common analyst, and 0 otherwise. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales
to the buyer to be converted into the firm level.

CAN f The between-firm component of CANft, which is the average CANft across all fiscal years for each firm.

CAUft 1 if the relationship has a common auditor at the audit firm level, and 0 otherwise. The variable is weighted by
the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm level.

CAU f The between-firm component of CAUft, which is the average CAUft across all fiscal years for each firm.

CDIft 1 if the relationship has a common director, and 0 otherwise. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales
to the buyer to be converted into the firm level.

CDI f The between-firm component of CDIft, which is the average CDIft across all fiscal years for each firm.

CII_ASYft The weighted average asymmetric institutional ownership that is calculated as the absolute difference between
supplier ownership and buyer ownership for each CII at the end of the last quarter of her shareholding in
year t. The weight is the ownership the CII holds in the supplier. It is 0 for buyer–supplier relationships
without CIIs. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm
level. We use its decile ranks for empirical tests.

CII ASY f The between-firm component of CII_ASYft, which is the average CII_ASYft across all fiscal years for each firm.

CII_COft The weighted average common institutional ownership that is calculated as the lower between supplier
ownership and buyer ownership for each CII at the end of the last quarter of her shareholding in year t. The
weight is the ownership the CII holds in the supplier. It is 0 for buyer–supplier relationships without CIIs.
The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm level. We use its
decile ranks for empirical tests.

CII CO f The between-firm component of CII_COft, which is the average CII_COft across all fiscal years for each firm.

CII_Dft 1 if the relationship has a common institutional investor who simultaneously holds the stock of the supplier
and buyer for more than two quarters in the fiscal year, and 0 otherwise. The variable is weighted by the
percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm level.

CII D f The between-firm component of CII_Dft, which is the average CII_Dft across all fiscal years for each firm.

CII_Longft The weighted indicator of a long-term CII within a buyer–supplier relationship. The weight is the ownership
the CII holds in the supplier. CIIs are long-term investors if their holding period of a buyer–supplier
relationship is longer than 2 years. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be
converted into firm level.

CII Long f The between-firm component of CII_Longft, which is the average CII_Longft across all fiscal years for each firm.

Dft Dividends, adjusted for inflation to 2011.

Debt ratioft The ratio of total debt over total assets.

Debt ratio f The between-firm component of Debt ratioft, which is the average Debt ratioft across all fiscal years for each
firm.

Direct ownershipit The stock ownership of one partner holds in the other.

DL_Dft 1 if one partner holds the stock of the other, and 0 otherwise. The variable is weighted by the percentage of
sales to the buyer to be converted into firm level.

696 CHEUNG ET AL.

Variables Definition

DL D f The between-firm component of DL_Dft, which is the average DL_Dft across all fiscal years for each firm.

Eft Earnings before extraordinary items, adjusted for inflation to 2011.

FYEAR Indicators for every fiscal year except the base year.

GPMft + 1 Gross profit margin at t + 1, calculated as (net sale − cost of goods sold)/net sale.

High dependenceft 1 if ADEPft is above the median of all firms in the same industry and year, and 0 otherwise.

Ift Interest expense, adjusted for inflation to 2011.

IND Indicators for every industry based on two-digit SIC code except the base industry.

Industry R&D Industry-level R&D expenditures divided by industry-level total assets. Industries are classified by two-digit SIC
codes. Missing value of R&D expenditures is treated as 0.

INSTft Total ownership of all institutional investors (including both CIIs and non-CIIs) at the quarter ending at or
before the fiscal year end date.

INST f The between-firm component of INSTft, which is the average INSTft across all fiscal years for each firm.

Lft Total debt/(total debt + market value of equity).

Mft Market value of equity (common shares outstanding * price at the end of the fiscal year).

NFft Total equity issuance minus repurchases plus debt issuance minus debt redemption.

RB Value-weighted return of the Fama–French size-BE/ME portfolio to which the firm belongs.

rft Realized stock return during fiscal year t.

ROSft Net income divided by net sales.

ROS f The between-firm component of ROSft, which is the average ROSft across all fiscal years for each firm.

Sales growthft The growth rate of net sales, calculated as (net sale at t − net sale at t − 1)/net sale at t.

Sales growth f The between-firm component of Sales growthft, which is the average Sales growthft across all fiscal years for each
firm.

Sizeft Natural logarithm of total assets.

Size f The between-firm component of Sizeft, which is the average Sizeft across all fiscal years for each firm.

ΔAft Change in inflation adjusted total assets from t − 1 to t.

ΔCANft Change in the status of common analysts between the supplier and buyer from t − 1 to t. The variable is
weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm level.

ΔCAUft Change in the status of common auditors between the supplier and buyer from t − 1 to t. The variable is
weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm level.

ΔCDIft Change in the status of common directors between the supplier and buyer from t − 1 to t. The variable is
weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm level.

ΔCII_ASYft Change in ownership asymmetry of CIIs from t − 1 to t. The calculation of ownership asymmetry is shown in
Section 5.5. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm
level. We then use its decile ranks for empirical tests.

ΔCII_COft Change in common ownership of CIIs from t − 1 to t. The calculation of common ownership is shown in
Section 5.5. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer to be converted into the firm
level. We then use its decile ranks for empirical tests.

ΔCII_Dft Change in the presence of CIIs from t − 1 to t. The variable is weighted by the percentage of sales to the buyer
to be converted into the firm level.

ΔDft Change in Dft from t − 1 to t.

ΔEft Change in Eft from t − 1 to t.

ΔINSTft Change in INSTft from t − 1 to t.

ΔIft Change in Ift from t − 1 to t.

ΔRDft Change in inflation adjusted R&D expenditures from t − 1 to t.
